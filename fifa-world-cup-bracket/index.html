Frequently Asked Questions

How does the 2026 World Cup knockout format work? ▼
The 2026 World Cup expanded to 48 teams. 12 group winners, 12 runners-up, and 8 best third-placed teams advanced to a Round of 32 knockout stage. From there it was a single-elimination bracket through Round of 16, Quarterfinals, Semifinals, and the Final — won by Spain 1–0 over Argentina.
How are the Round of 32 matchups decided? ▼
FIFA assigned pre-set bracket paths. For example, Group A winner faced a third-placed team from Groups C, D, F, H, or I, while Group B winner faced a third-placed team from E, F, I, or J. The tournament has concluded; Spain lifted the trophy after beating Argentina in the Final.
Can I save my bracket predictions? ▼
Yes! Click the Share button to generate a URL with all your picks encoded. Bookmark that link or send it to friends. When you open the link, your bracket loads automatically.
How many teams qualify for the knockout stage? ▼
32 teams advance from the group stage: 12 group winners, 12 runners-up, and 8 best third-placed finishers. That's about 67% of the 48-team field making it through.
Where is the 2026 World Cup being held? ▼
The tournament is hosted across three North American countries: the United States, Canada, and Mexico. A total of 16 venues across the three nations hosted matches, with the Final at MetLife Stadium in New Jersey, where Spain beat Argentina 1–0.
Is this the official FIFA bracket? ▼
This tool follows the official FIFA bracket template for the 2026 World Cup knockout stage. The tournament has concluded: Spain won the title, beating Argentina 1–0 in the Final. Use the bracket to review how the knockout played out.
