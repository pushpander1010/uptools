❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the FIFA World Cup Golden Boot? +
The FIFA World Cup Golden Boot is awarded to the top goal scorer of each FIFA World Cup tournament. If multiple players finish with the same number of goals, assists are used as the tiebreaker, followed by fewer minutes played.
Who won the Golden Boot in previous World Cups? +
2022: Kylian Mbappé (8 goals, France) • 2018: Harry Kane (6 goals, England) • 2014: James Rodríguez (6 goals, Colombia) • 2010: Thomas Müller (5 goals, Germany) • 2006: Miroslav Klose (5 goals, Germany) • 2026: Kylian Mbappé (10 goals, France).
How is the Golden Boot winner determined if there is a tie? +
If two or more players finish with the same number of goals, FIFA uses assists as the first tiebreaker. If still tied, the player who played fewer minutes receives the award.
How many goals are typically needed to win the Golden Boot? +
In recent tournaments, the Golden Boot winner has scored between 5-8 goals. The record is 13 goals by Just Fontaine in 1958. The average winning tally in the modern era (since 1998) is around 6-7 goals.
When will the 2026 Golden Boot winner be decided? +
Kylian Mbappé of France won the 2026 Golden Boot with 10 goals, ahead of Lionel Messi (Argentina, 8). The race was decided after the Final on July 19, 2026, which Spain won 1–0 against Argentina.
