Tournament Overview

The 2026 FIFA World Cup was the first edition with 48 teams and 104 matches across 16 venues in the USA, Canada, and Mexico. It ran from June 11 to July 19, 2026, and concluded with Spain beating Argentina 1–0 in the Final at MetLife Stadium to win their second title. The new format featured 12 groups of 4 (Groups A-L), with the top 2 from each group plus the 8 best third-placed teams advancing to a Round of 32.

Frequently Asked Questions

When does the FIFA World Cup 2026 start and end? ▼
The tournament kicked off on June 11, 2026 with the opening match and concluded with the Final on July 19, 2026 at MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, New Jersey — won 1–0 by Spain against Argentina.
How many matches are in the 2026 World Cup? ▼
There were 104 matches total — 72 group stage matches, 16 in the Round of 32, 8 in the Round of 16, 4 quarterfinals, 2 semifinals, the third-place match, and the Final, won 1–0 by Spain against Argentina.
Which countries are hosting the 2026 World Cup? ▼
The United States, Canada, and Mexico are co-hosting the 2026 FIFA World Cup — the first time three nations have jointly hosted the tournament. Matches are spread across 16 venues in major cities including Los Angeles, New York/New Jersey, Mexico City, Toronto, Vancouver, and more.
What is the new format for 2026? ▼
The 2026 World Cup expands from 32 to 48 teams. Teams are divided into 12 groups of 4. The top 2 from each group plus the 8 best third-placed teams advance to a Round of 32 — the first time a World Cup has had a 32-team knockout stage.
Where is the 2026 World Cup Final? ▼
The Final was played at MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, New Jersey (New York metro area) on July 19, 2026 at 7:00 PM ET. Spain won 1–0 against Argentina to claim their second World Cup title.
How can I buy tickets for 2026 World Cup matches? ▼
Official tickets are sold through FIFA's ticketing portal. Ticket sales typically open in phases, starting with a random draw for group stage matches. Check FIFA.com for official announcements. Be wary of resale sites — only purchase through authorized channels.

Do's & Don'ts for World Cup 2026

Do's

  • Book accommodation and transport well in advance — host cities will fill up fast
  • Apply for official tickets through FIFA's portal during the sales windows
  • Check visa requirements early — you may need a US ESTA, Canadian eTA, or Mexican visa
  • Arrive at venues at least 2 hours before kick-off for security checks
  • Use this schedule to plan which matches you want to attend each day

Don'ts

  • Don't buy tickets from unofficial resellers — FIFA tickets are digital and non-transferable
  • Don't book non-refundable travel before your match tickets are confirmed
  • Don't forget to check if matches are in different cities on the same day — travel between venues takes time
  • Don't bring prohibited items to stadiums (large bags, bottles, flares, etc.)
  • Don't assume all matches are shown on free-to-air TV — check broadcast rights in your region
