About UPI ID Validation

This tool validates UPI IDs by checking the format, username rules, and bank domain. It supports all recognized Indian UPI handles including bank-specific and third-party app domains.

📘 How UPI IDs work
  • UPI IDs follow the format: username@bank (e.g., john@okicici).
  • The username can contain letters, numbers, periods, hyphens, and underscores.
  • The domain (after @) identifies the bank or UPI service provider.
  • UPI is regulated by NPCI (National Payments Corporation of India).
🏦 Common UPI domains
  • Bank domains: @okicici, @okaxis, @oksbi, @okhdfcbank, @okbankofindia
  • Third-party: @ybl (PhonePe), @paytm, @googlepay, @razorpay
  • UPI 2.0: @ibl, @unionbank, @bankofindia, @centralbank
  • This tool supports 200+ recognized UPI domains.

📐 How This Tool Works

The validator checks three components of a UPI ID:

  1. Username: Must be 1-50 characters, alphanumeric with . - _ allowed
  2. @ symbol: Required separator between username and domain
  3. Domain: Must be a recognized UPI bank or app handle from our database of 200+ domains
