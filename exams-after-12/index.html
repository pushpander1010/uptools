Entrance Exams After Class 12

This page focuses only on exams you can target directly after Class 12. Postgraduate exams such as CAT are intentionally excluded.

Stream-awareEligibility-focusedEvergreen overview

JEE Main / Advanced

Engineering admissions for PCM students.

Who it fits: Students aiming for B.Tech and allied engineering programs.
Core subjects: Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics.
Typical route: NITs, IIITs, IITs, and other engineering colleges.

NEET UG

Primary medical entrance route for PCB students.

Who it fits: MBBS, BDS, AYUSH, and many allied health programs.
Core subjects: Physics, Chemistry, Biology.
Typical route: Medical, dental, and health-sciences colleges.

CUET UG

Broad undergraduate entry route across many universities.

Who it fits: Students targeting BA, BCom, BSc, and many domain-based UG courses.
Core subjects: Domain papers chosen by course requirements.
Typical route: Central universities and participating institutions.

CLAT / AILET

Law entrances for integrated five-year law programs.

Who it fits: Students planning BA LLB, BBA LLB, or similar law tracks.
Core areas: English, reasoning, GK, legal aptitude, and basic math.
Typical route: National law universities and leading law schools.

NDA

Defence path for students interested in armed forces careers.

Who it fits: Candidates interested in Army, Navy, or Air Force officer training.
Core areas: Mathematics, general ability, SSB interview stages.
Typical route: National Defence Academy and service-based progression.

NIFT / NID / UCEED

Design-focused entrances for fashion, product, and visual design.

Who it fits: Students with strong portfolio, creativity, and design aptitude.
Core areas: Drawing, observation, design thinking, aptitude, and studio tests.
Typical route: Fashion, industrial, UX, visual communication, and design schools.

How To Choose

  • Match the exam to your stream first, then to your target course.
  • Check subject eligibility before spending time on preparation.
  • Keep one broad option such as CUET UG alongside specialized entrances.
  • Use college cutoffs and course outcomes together, not exam brand alone.
