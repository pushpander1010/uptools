Frequently Asked Questions

What is home equity?

Home equity is the portion of your home that you own outright. It's calculated as: Current Home Value - Outstanding Mortgage Balance. For example, if your home is worth $400K and you owe $250K, you have $150K in equity.

How do I build home equity?

Build equity by: 1) Making mortgage payments (reduces loan balance), 2) Home appreciation (increases value), 3) Making extra principal payments, 4) Home improvements that increase value.

What is a good amount of home equity?

20% equity is the minimum to avoid PMI. 50%+ equity provides financial flexibility. Most lenders allow borrowing up to 80-85% of home value, so maintaining 15-20% equity is recommended.
