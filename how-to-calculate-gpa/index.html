How to Calculate GPA

A simple, step-by-step guide to calculating GPA on any scale — plus free calculators that do it for you.

Step 2 — Multiply by credits

For each course: grade points × course credits = quality points. Add all quality points, then divide by total credits attempted. That's your GPA.

Example: A (3 cr) + B (4 cr) + A (2 cr) → (4×3)+(3×4)+(4×2) = 12+12+8 = 32 ÷ 9 credits = 3.56 GPA.

Step 3 — CGPA (cumulative)

CGPA = total quality points across all semesters ÷ total credits. Or simply average your semester GPAs weighted by credits. Use our CGPA Calculator for instant conversion.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate weighted GPA?

Add a point for honors/AP/IB courses (A=5 instead of 4), then run the same quality-points ÷ credits formula.

GPA vs CGPA?

GPA = one term; CGPA = all terms combined. CGPA is what colleges and employers usually ask for.

Is a 3.5 GPA good?

Yes — 3.5 is roughly an A-/B+ average, strong for most universities and scholarships.
