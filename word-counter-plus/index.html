About Word Counter

This advanced word counter provides comprehensive text analysis including word count, character count, sentence count, paragraph count, reading time, and readability scores. Perfect for writers, students, and professionals.

📘 How readability scores work
  • Flesch Reading Ease: 0-100 scale. Higher = easier to read. 60-70 is standard.
  • Grade Level: US school grade level. 8th grade = standard for most content.
  • Reading Age: The age group that can easily read the text.
  • Reading Time: Based on 200 words per minute average reading speed.
📊 Readability scale
  • 90-100: Very Easy (5th grade) — Easy for any reader
  • 80-89: Easy (6th grade) — Conversational English
  • 70-79: Fairly Easy (7th grade) —适合大多数读者
  • 60-69: Standard (8th-9th grade) —适合大多数读者
  • 50-59: Fairly Hard (10th-12th grade) — High school level
  • 30-49: Hard (College) — Academic writing
  • 0-29: Very Hard (Graduate) — Professional/academic

📐 How This Tool Works

This tool analyzes text in real-time using JavaScript. Key metrics:

  • Words: Split by whitespace, excluding empty strings
  • Characters: Total characters including spaces
  • Sentences: Counted by splitting on sentence-ending punctuation (. ! ?)
  • Paragraphs: Counted by splitting on double newlines
  • Reading Time: words ÷ 200 = minutes
  • Flesch Score: 206.835 - 1.015 × (total words / total sentences) - 84.6 × (total syllables / total words)

✅ Do's and ❌ Don'ts for Writing

✅ Do's

  • ✅ Do aim for a readability score of 60-70 for general content.
  • ✅ Do keep sentences under 20 words for better readability.
  • ✅ Do use short paragraphs (3-4 sentences) for online content.
  • ✅ Do proofread your text before publishing.

❌ Don'ts

  • ❌ Don't write sentences that are too long (over 30 words).
  • ❌ Don't use jargon without explaining it.
  • ❌ Don't ignore readability scores for web content.
  • ❌ Don't forget to check spelling and grammar.
