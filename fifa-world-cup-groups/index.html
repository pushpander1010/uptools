FAQ

When did the 2026 FIFA World Cup start?
The 2026 FIFA World Cup ran from June 11 to July 19, 2026. It was the first edition to feature 48 teams across 12 groups of 4, hosted across Canada, Mexico, and the United States. Spain won the tournament, beating Argentina 1–0 in the Final.
How are teams ranked in the group standings?
Teams are ranked by points (3 for a win, 1 for a draw, 0 for a loss), then goal difference (GD), then goals scored (GF). If still tied, head-to-head results, disciplinary points, and a drawing of lots may be used.
How many teams qualify from each group?
The top two teams from each of the 12 groups advanced to the Round of 32. The 24 qualified teams were then joined by the 8 best third-placed teams to complete the knockout stage.
Who won the 2026 World Cup?
Spain won their second World Cup title, defeating Argentina 1–0 in the Final at MetLife Stadium on July 19, 2026. Kylian Mbappé of France won the Golden Boot with 10 goals.
Is this page updated in real time?
The tournament has concluded. All group fixtures and standings shown reflect the final, official results from FIFA.

This page covers the 2026 edition only. For historical results, visit FIFA's official archives or check our related football tools section below.
