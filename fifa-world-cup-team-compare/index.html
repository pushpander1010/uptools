Frequently Asked Questions

Where does the comparison data come from?

Data is compiled from official FIFA rankings, World Cup historical records, and current squad information. All data is for informational purposes.

How is the radar chart calculated?

Each attribute (Attack, Defense, Midfield, Experience, Depth) is scored on a 0-100 scale based on FIFA ranking position, historical World Cup performance, and squad quality indicators.

What does the head-to-head record show?

It shows the historical match results between the two selected teams, including total matches played, wins for each side, draws, and goals scored.

Can I compare any two teams?

Yes! You can compare any two of the 48 qualified nations for FIFA World Cup 2026, regardless of confederation.

⚙️ How the Team Comparison Works

The comparison tool analyzes each team across multiple dimensions to provide a comprehensive side-by-side evaluation:

  • Attack Rating — based on attacking players, recent goalscoring form, and historical offensive output in World Cup tournaments.
  • Defense Rating — based on defensive solidity, goalkeeper quality, and clean sheet records.
  • Midfield Rating — based on midfield creativity, ball retention, and playmaking ability.
  • Experience Rating — based on tournament experience, average squad age, and caps per player.
  • Squad Depth — based on bench strength, number of top-league players, and versatility.
