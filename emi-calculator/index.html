About this EMI Calculator

This EMI calculator uses the standard reducing-balance formula to compute your monthly payment. Results are instant and calculated in your browser. Nothing is stored or uploaded.

📘 How to use
  • Enter loan amount (₹), annual interest (%), and tenure (months or years).
  • Click Calculate to see EMI, total interest, and total payment.
  • Optionally enter a prepayment month and amount to reduce total interest.
  • Use Compare fields to evaluate a second loan offer.
  • Export the full schedule as CSV or share a permalink.
❓ Formula

EMI = P × r × (1 + r)n ÷ [(1 + r)n − 1], where:

  • P = Loan principal
  • r = Monthly rate = (annual% ÷ 12 ÷ 100)
  • n = Number of months

If r = 0, EMI = P ÷ n.

✅ Do's and ❌ Don'ts for Loan EMI Planning

✅ Do's

  • ✅ Do keep total EMI under 40% of monthly take-home income.
  • ✅ Do use prepayment to reduce total interest — even small amounts help.
  • ✅ Do compare at least 2-3 lenders before choosing a loan.
  • ✅ Do factor in processing fees and prepayment charges.
  • ✅ Do use the amortization schedule to plan finances month by month.
  • ✅ Do build an emergency fund covering 3-6 months of EMI before borrowing.

❌ Don'ts

  • ❌ Don't choose tenure just for the lowest EMI — it may cost more interest.
  • ❌ Don't ignore the impact of rate changes on floating-rate loans.
  • ❌ Don't prepay without checking for prepayment penalties.
  • ❌ Don't take on new debt before repaying existing EMIs.
  • ❌ Don't forget insurance and processing fees in total cost.
  • ❌ Don't share loan details with untrusted third-party apps.
📐 How This EMI Calculator Works

Formula: EMI = P × r × (1 + r)n ÷ [(1 + r)n − 1]

  • P = Loan principal (amount borrowed)
  • r = Monthly interest rate = annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100
  • n = Total number of months

Worked Example

₹10,00,000 at 9% p.a. for 240 months:

r = 0.09 ÷ 12 = 0.0075

EMI ≈ ₹8,997 | Total interest ≈ ₹11,59,280 | Total ≈ ₹21,59,280

Example with Prepayment

Prepay ₹2,00,000 in month 12 → remaining principal drops, tenure shortens, and total interest saved can be over ₹1,00,000.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good loan tenure?

Shorter tenures reduce total interest but increase EMI. Pick an EMI that fits your budget.

Fixed vs floating rates?

Floating rates are usually lower but can change. Fixed rates offer certainty but are higher.

Can I compare two loans?

Yes. Fill in the Compare fields to see EMI and total interest side-by-side.

How does prepayment reduce interest?

Prepayment reduces outstanding principal, so less interest accrues for all remaining months.
