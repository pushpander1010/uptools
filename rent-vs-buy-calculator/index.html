Frequently Asked Questions

Is it better to rent or buy a home?

It depends on your situation. Buying is better if you plan to stay 5+ years, have stable income, and can afford the down payment. Renting is better if you value flexibility, don't want maintenance costs, or expect to move soon.

What is the 5% rule for rent vs buy?

The 5% rule states that if annual rent is less than 5% of the home's purchase price, renting is likely cheaper. For example, if a home costs $400K, annual rent under $20K ($1,667/month) favors renting.

What costs are included in buying a home?

Buying costs include: down payment, closing costs (2-5%), mortgage interest, property taxes, homeowners insurance, HOA fees, maintenance (1-2% of home value annually), and opportunity cost of down payment.
